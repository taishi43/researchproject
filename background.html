Background

Who are Asian Americans?

Much of the existing academic work on "Asian Americans" encounters the same problems it seems; firstly, there is too much diversity, whether it be in ethnicity, socioeconomic status, recency of immigration, or a variety of other factors, as evidenced by the monolithic grouping done in this Pew article with little respect towards how it may have disproportionately affected certain ethnic groups. Secondly, the population is considered too small for a statistically significant sample size as evidenced by this article.
